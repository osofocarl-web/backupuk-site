Welcome to Module 7 of the Level 4 Foundational Bible School Certificate Program.

If you possess a cure for a terminal disease, it is grossly negligent, and arguably criminal, to keep it a secret. Humanity is suffering from the terminal spiritual disease of sin, and the Church holds the exclusive cure: the Gospel of Jesus Christ. Evangelism is not a spiritual gift reserved for a select few extroverts; it is the universal mandate assigned to every born-again believer. 

### 1. The Great Commission
Christ's final words on earth were not a suggestion; they were an absolute directive. This directive, known as the Great Commission, establishes the overarching mission statement for the Church across all eras.

*"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age."* (Matthew 28:19-20)

**Notice three crucial elements here:**
1.  **It is proactive:** "Go." We are not called to wait defensively inside our church buildings for the lost to magically show up. We are directed to intentionally infiltrate the culture.
2.  **It is reproductive:** "Make disciples." We are not just trying to get people to say a quick prayer; we are called to shepherd them into an ongoing lifestyle of obedience to Jesus.
3.  **It is global:** "All nations." The gospel has no geographical, socioeconomic, or racial boundaries. 

### 2. What Is The Gospel?
To effectively evangelize, we must accurately define the "Good News" (which is what the Greek word *evangelion* means). Many Christians struggle to articulate what they actually believe when pressed.

The Gospel we preach must contain these non-negotiable pillars:
*   **The Holiness of God:** God is infinitely pure, just, and the creator of all things.
*   **The Problem of Sin:** Every human being has broken God's law, resulting in total spiritual death and eternal separation from the Father. Good deeds cannot bridge this gap.
*   **The Person of Christ:** Jesus is fully God and fully man. He lived a sinless life, absorbing the terrifying wrath of God's justice on the cross as our substitute, and rose physically from the dead to conquer the grave.
*   **The Response Required:** We must respond exclusively through repentance (turning from our dead works and sin) and faith (placing all of our trust in Christ's finished work). 

### 3. The Role of the Holy Spirit in Evangelism
One of the most paralyzing fears believers face in evangelism is the fear of failure or rejection. We often think, "What if I don't know the right scriptures? What if I say it wrong?"

This fear stems from a profound theological misunderstanding: **It is not your job to save anyone.**
Only the Holy Spirit can save a soul. Only the Holy Spirit can convict a heart of its sin, its need for righteousness, and the reality of coming judgment (John 16:8). 

Your job is simply to be a faithful witness. A witness in a courtroom is never expected to be the lawyer or the judge; they are merely required to accurately testify to what they have personally seen, heard, and experienced. You plant the seed; God is exclusively responsible for the harvest (1 Corinthians 3:6-7).

### 4. Lifestyle Evangelism vs. Proclamation
Effective evangelism operates dynamically through two synchronized channels: the life you live and the words you speak.

*"In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven."* (Matthew 5:16)

**1. The Witness of the Life:** A transformed, holy, joyous life operates as a blinding beacon in a dark world. When you display integrity at work, peace in the midst of tragedy, and supernatural love toward difficult people, you provoke questions. Your behavior prepares the soil.

**2. The Witness of the Lips:** However, living a good life is never enough. No one has ever been saved by merely observing someone else's manners. Romans 10:14 states: *"How, then, can they call on the one they have not believed in? And how can they believe in the one of whom they have not heard? And how can they hear without someone preaching to them?"* A holy life earns you the right to be heard, but you must eventually open your mouth and proclaim the Name of Jesus.

#### Practical Application for Today
If you have never led someone to Christ, you are missing out on the most supernatural joy known to humanity.
1.  **Draft Your Testimony:** Take 10 minutes to write out your personal story in three short acts: My life before Christ, how I encountered Christ, and how my life has changed since. Practice saying it in under 3 minutes.
2.  **Pray for the "Oikos":** Your *oikos* is the Greek term for your immediate sphere of influence (family, friends, coworkers). Choose three unbelieving people in your *oikos* and begin praying actively for their salvation every single day. Ask the Holy Spirit to open physical doors for spiritual conversations.
