Welcome to Module 3 of the Level 4 Foundational Bible School Certificate Program. 

One of the most foundational doctrines of the Christian faith is repentance. In fact, Hebrews 6:1 lists "repentance from dead works" as the very first elementary principle of Christ. Without true biblical repentance, there is no salvation, no transformation, and no relationship with God.

### 1. What Repentance Is Not
In modern culture, the concept of repentance has been heavily diluted. It is often confused with mere emotional reactions or behavioral modification. To understand true repentance, we must first address what it is not:
*   **It is not just feeling guilty:** Judas Iscariot felt intense remorse and guilt after betraying Jesus, yet he did not repent (Matthew 27:3-5). Guilt is a feeling; repentance is an action.
*   **It is not simply apologizing:** Saying "I'm sorry" without a corresponding change in direction is just regret. 
*   **It is not penance:** We cannot "pay God back" for our sins through religious acts or self-punishment. 

### 2. The Biblical Definition (Metanoia)
In the New Testament Greek, the word used for repentance is *metanoia*. This word breaks down into two parts:
*   *Meta:* After or implies a change.
*   *Noia:* The mind or intellect.

Therefore, biblical repentance means **a radical change of mind**. It is a profound shift in how you view God, how you view your own sin, and how you view the world. 

When your mind truly changes about an object, your behavior toward that object inevitably changes. If you genuinely change your mind about the safety of an oncoming train, you will immediately step off the tracks. In the same way, true repentance *(a change of mind)* will always produce a 180-degree turnaround in your lifestyle *(a change of direction)*.

*"Produce fruit in keeping with repentance."* (Matthew 3:8)

### 3. Repentance from "Dead Works"
Hebrews 6 specifically commands repentance from "dead works." What are dead works?

1.  **Works of the Flesh:** These are the obvious acts of sinful rebellion against God's law (Galatians 5:19-21: sexual immorality, impurity, idolatry, hatred, discord, jealousy, fits of rage). 
2.  **Self-Righteous Religious Works:** These are "good deeds" performed with the intention of earning God's favor or proving our own righteousness. Isaiah 64:6 declares that *"all our righteous acts are like filthy rags"* when presented as payment for our sin.

We must repent not only of our worst sins but also of our best efforts to save ourselves. We must abandon any reliance on our own goodness and throw ourselves completely upon the mercy of Christ.

### 4. The Gift of Godly Sorrow
While repentance is not merely an emotion, true biblical repentance is often triggered by "godly sorrow." The Apostle Paul draws a sharp distinction between godly sorrow and worldly sorrow in 2 Corinthians 7:10:

*"Godly sorrow brings repentance that leads to salvation and leaves no regret, but worldly sorrow brings death."*

*   **Worldly Sorrow:** Being sad that you got caught. Being embarrassed by the consequences of your sin. It is focused on the *self*.
*   **Godly Sorrow:** Being devastated that your sin grieved the heart of a holy and loving God. It is focused on *God*.

#### Practical Application for Today
Repentance is not just a one-time event at the moment of salvation; it is the daily posture of a healthy believer.
1.  **Keep a Short Account:** When the Holy Spirit convicts you of sin, do not defend it, minimize it, or hide it. Agree with God immediately (1 John 1:9), turn from the behavior, and run back to His grace.
2.  **Examine Your Motives:** Are you relying on "dead works" (church attendance, giving, moral behavior) to make you feel right with God? Repent of self-righteousness and rest entirely in the finished work of Jesus on the cross.
