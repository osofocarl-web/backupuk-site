Welcome to Module 10 of the Level 4 Foundational Bible School Certificate Program.

Eschatology is the theology of "last things." While Christians can hold differing views on the specific timeline of the end times (pre-tribulation, post-millennial, etc.), the core tenets of Christ's physical return, the resurrection of the dead, and the final judgment are absolute, non-negotiable pillars of orthodox faith. Studying eschatology should not produce fear or wild conspiracy theories; it should produce profound hope and urgent holiness.

### 1. The Promised Return of the King (The Parousia)
Jesus Christ did not end His story climbing into the clouds. He made an unbreakable vow that He would physically, visibly return to earth to consummate the Kingdom of God and execute final justice.

*"For the Son of Man is going to come in his Father’s glory with his angels, and then he will reward each person according to what they have done."* (Matthew 16:27)

When He came the first time, He came quietly as a suffering servant to bear the wrath of God on a cross. When He returns the second time, He will split the sky as a conquering Warrior-King to dispense the wrath of God upon the unrepentant (Revelation 19). Every eye will see Him (Revelation 1:7).

### 2. The Resurrection of the Dead
Death is not the end of human existence. The Bible emphatically teaches a physical resurrection of every human being who has ever lived.

*"Do not be amazed at this, for a time is coming when all who are in their graves will hear his voice and come out—those who have done what is good will rise to live, and those who have done what is evil will rise to be condemned."* (John 5:28-29)

Believers will be resurrected with glorified, imperishable bodies exactly like Christ's resurrected body, designed for eternal life perfectly free from sickness, sin, and decay (1 Corinthians 15).

### 3. The Final Judgment (The Bema and the Great White Throne)
The Bible speaks of two distinct judgments.

**1. The Bema Seat of Christ (For Believers):**
*"For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, so that each of us may receive what is due us for the things done while in the body, whether good or bad."* (2 Corinthians 5:10)
This is not a judgment of salvation—that was settled permanently on the cross. This is an evaluation of stewardship. Believers will be rewarded based on their faithfulness, obedience, and how they utilized their resources to advance the Kingdom.

**2. The Great White Throne Judgment (For the Unrepentant):**
*"Then I saw a great white throne and him who was seated on it... And I saw the dead, great and small, standing before the throne, and books were opened... If anyone’s name was not found written in the book of life, they were thrown into the lake of fire."* (Revelation 20:11-15)
This is the terrifying, final execution of justice upon every person who rejected the grace of Jesus Christ. 

### 4. The New Heavens and New Earth
The ultimate goal of salvation is not for disembodied spirits to float on clouds playing harps. The ultimate goal is the complete redemption and recreation of the physical cosmos. 

*"Then I saw ‘a new heaven and a new earth,’ for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away... And I heard a loud voice from the throne saying, 'Look! God’s dwelling place is now among the people, and he will dwell with them. They will be his people, and God himself will be with them and be their God.'"* (Revelation 21:1-3)

God will permanently lift the curse of sin off the physical universe. Heaven and Earth will merge into one glorious, perfected reality where we will live, work, rule, and worship in the unmediated presence of the Father and the Son for all eternity.

#### Practical Application for Today
Eschatology is the fuel for urgency and purity.
1.  **Live with Urgency:** If you truly believe Jesus could return at any moment, and that those without Christ face an eternal hell, your evangelism should be marked by absolute desperation. Who are you warning today?
2.  **Live with Purity:** *"Everyone who has this hope in him purifies himself, just as he is pure."* (1 John 3:3). The reality of standing before the Bema Seat of Christ should radically govern what you look at, how you speak, and how you manage your time right now.
