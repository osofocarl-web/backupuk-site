Welcome to Module 9 of the Level 4 Foundational Bible School Certificate Program.

Money and material possessions are competing spiritual masters. In fact, Jesus spoke more about money and possessions than He did about heaven and hell combined! He understood that the way a person handles their finances is the ultimate litmus test for the actual condition of their heart.

### 1. The Principle of Absolute Ownership
The foundational doctrine of Biblical stewardship is the understanding that God owns literally everything. 

*"The earth is the Lord’s, and everything in it, the world, and all who live in it."* (Psalm 24:1)

You do not own your house, your bank account, your car, or even your own body. You are merely a temporary manager—a steward—dispatched by the CEO of the Universe to manage His assets for His maximum return. Once this paradigm shift occurs, it completely destroys the spirit of poverty and the spirit of greed. The pressure is off you to provide, and the mandate is onto you to manage optimally.

### 2. The Litmus Test of Managing the Small Things
Because God is the owner, He is intensely observing how we handle the small amounts of capital He entrusts to us. Jesus explained the law of stewardship in Luke 16:10-11:

*"Whoever can be trusted with very little can also be trusted with much, and whoever is dishonest with very little will also be dishonest with much. So if you have not been trustworthy in handling worldly wealth, who will trust you with true riches?"*

If we squander God's material resources on selfish, fleeting desires, why would He ever entrust us with eternal spiritual authority (the "true riches")? 

### 3. The Tithe: The Baseline of Trust
In the Old Testament, God established the tithe (the first 10% of all increase) as a holy mechanism for funding His house and proving the obedience of His people.
*"Bring the whole tithe into the storehouse, that there may be food in my house. Test me in this," says the Lord Almighty, "and see if I will not throw open the floodgates of heaven and pour out so much blessing that there will not be room enough to store it."* (Malachi 3:10)

While we live under grace and not the Mosaic Law, Jesus Himself affirmed tithing in Matthew 23:23. Tithing is the mathematical training ground for generosity. It breaks the gravitational pull of materialism. If you cannot trust God mathematically with 10% of your income, you will never trust Him with the more difficult aspects of your life. 

### 4. Radical Grace-Driven Generosity
The New Testament standard for giving does not end at 10%. The tithe is merely the training wheels; the New Testament standard is radical, grace-driven generosity.

*"Each of you should give what you have decided in your heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver."* (2 Corinthians 9:7)

The Macedonia church modeled this profoundly. Even in extreme poverty, they begged for the privilege of giving beyond their ability to support the mission of the Gospel (2 Corinthians 8:1-4). When your heart has been overwhelmed by the lavish generosity of Christ on the cross (He gave everything), your natural gravitational pull will be to give lavishly of your finances, your time, and your energy.

#### Practical Application for Today
Stewardship requires strategic planning, not emotional reacting.
1.  **Draft a Kingdom Budget:** You cannot steward what you do not track. Create a written budget for your finances. Before you allocate a single penny to housing, food, or entertainment, allocate the "first fruits" (the tithe) to your Local Church. 
2.  **Audit Your Assets:** Take an inventory of your time and talents. Are you using your skills exclusively to climb the corporate ladder, or are you aggressively investing them into serving the Body of Christ?
