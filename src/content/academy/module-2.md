Welcome to Module 2 of the Level 4 Foundational Bible School Certificate Program.

Water baptism is one of the most visible and joyous sacraments of the Christian church. It is an outward expression of an inward reality. While baptism itself does not save you—only faith in Christ does that (Ephesians 2:8)—it is an essential act of obedience that every new believer is commanded to undergo.

### 1. The Command of Christ
Before ascending into heaven, Jesus gave His followers the Great Commission. He did not merely suggest baptism; He commanded it as part of the disciple-making process.

*"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."* (Matthew 28:19)

Throughout the Book of Acts, we see that the immediate response of individuals who received the Gospel was to be baptized.
*   **The Day of Pentecost:** When 3,000 people were saved, they were baptized the very same day (Acts 2:41).
*   **The Ethiopian Eunuch:** Upon hearing the Gospel from Philip, the eunuch immediately halted his chariot when he saw water, declaring, *"Look, here is water. What can stand in the way of my being baptized?"* (Acts 8:36)

### 2. The Spiritual Symbolism (Immersion)
In the New Testament, the Greek word for baptize is *baptizo*, which literally means "to plunge, dip, or immerse." Water baptism is a profound visual sermon portraying the death, burial, and resurrection of Jesus Christ, and our identification with Him.

Paul explains this powerful imagery in Romans 6:3-4:
*"Or don't you know that all of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death? We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life."*

**What does this mean practically?**
1.  **Going under the water:** This symbolizes the death and burial of your old, sinful nature. You are publicly declaring that your old self was crucified with Christ.
2.  **Coming up out of the water:** This symbolizes your resurrection into a new creation. You are raised to walk in the "newness of life."

### 3. Circumcision of the Heart
In the Old Testament, physical circumcision was the required boundary marker that identified a person as belonging to the covenant community of Israel. Under the New Covenant, the Apostle Paul likens water baptism to a spiritual circumcision.

*"In him you were also circumcised with a circumcision not performed by human hands. Your whole self ruled by the flesh was put off when you were circumcised by Christ, having been buried with him in baptism, in which you were also raised with him through your faith in the working of God, who raised him from the dead."* (Colossians 2:11-12)

Water baptism publicly marks you as a citizen of the Kingdom of Heaven and cuts away the spiritual dominion of the flesh over your life.

### 4. Who Should Be Baptized?
Biblical baptism requires a conscious decision of faith. Therefore, the biblical sequence is always:
1.  **Hear the Gospel**
2.  **Repent and Believe**
3.  **Be Baptized**

This is why we practice "Believer's Baptism." An individual must be old enough to understand the Gospel, recognize their sin, and make a personal volition to trust Christ before being baptized.

#### Practical Application for Today
If you have placed your faith in Jesus Christ but have not yet been baptized, your next step of spiritual obedience is clear.
1.  **Do not delay:** Like the early church in Acts, baptism should follow conversion as quickly as practically possible. 
2.  **Celebrate the covenant:** Baptism is a public wedding ring. It lets the entire spiritual and physical world know that you now belong exclusively to Jesus Christ. It is a moment of profound victory.
