Welcome to Module 4 of the Level 4 Foundational Bible School Certificate Program. 

If repentance is the act of turning *away* from dead works, then faith is the act of turning *towards* God. Faith is the overarching currency of heaven; it is the necessary mechanism by which we receive everything God has provided for us through grace. 

*"And without faith it is impossible to please God, because anyone who comes to him must believe that he exists and that he rewards those who earnestly seek him."* (Hebrews 11:6)

### 1. What is Biblical Faith?
Modern culture often defines faith as a blind leap into the dark, or a hopeful wish that something might turn out okay. However, biblical faith is exactly the opposite.

Hebrews 11:1 provides the definitive description:
*"Now faith is confidence in what we hope for and assurance about what we do not see."*

Faith is not a feeling; it is an active, unshakeable confidence built upon a known foundation. That foundation is the character, the nature, and the spoken Word of God. Biblical faith is acting like God told the truth, regardless of surrounding circumstances or sensory evidence to the contrary.

### 2. The Mechanics of Faith: How Does It Come?
You cannot simply "muster up" faith through intense concentration or emotional hype. Faith has a specific origin source.

*"Consequently, faith comes from hearing the message, and the message is heard through the word about Christ."* (Romans 10:17)

Faith is the automatic byproduct of exposing born-again human spirits to the living Word of God. The more you immerse yourself in Scripture, the more your mind is renewed, and the more faith naturally rises within you. Conversely, if you starve yourself of the Word of God, you will find yourself overwhelmed by doubt and anxiety.

### 3. Faith vs. Presumption
It is critically important to distinguish between genuine faith and reckless presumption. 
*   **Faith** is trusting and acting upon what God has explicitly promised or commanded in His Word. 
*   **Presumption** is deciding what *you* want to happen, demanding that God do it, and calling it "faith." 

Genuine faith is always anchored to the revealed will of God. If God has not spoken on a matter—either through general principle in Scripture or through specific confirmation of the Holy Spirit—we cannot exercise biblical faith regarding it; we can only exercise hope. This is why 1 John 5:14 states: *"This is the confidence we have in approaching God: that if we ask anything according to his will, he hears us."*

### 4. Faith Works Through Love
A common pitfall for believers is treating faith like a mechanical formula or a magical incantation. "If I just say the right words enough times, God has to do it." However, faith is fundamentally relational. It operates within the context of an intimate relationship with the Father.

The Apostle Paul reveals the spiritual engine that actually powers faith:
*"For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision has any value. The only thing that counts is faith expressing itself through love."* (Galatians 5:6)

If your heart is harboring bitterness, unforgiveness, or selfishness, your faith will be short-circuited. A deep, abiding revelation of God's perfect love for you, coupled with your love for Him and others, is the necessary spiritual climate for faith to thrive.

#### Practical Application for Today
Faith is a spiritual muscle. If it is not exercised, it will atrophy. But when put to the test, it grows exponentially stronger.
1.  **Audit Your Spiritual Diet:** Are you spending more time listening to the news, worldly philosophy, or anxious predictions than you are the Word of God? You cannot harbor a diet of fear and expect to produce faith. Protect your spiritual intake aggressively.
2.  **Act On What You Know:** Faith requires corresponding action (James 2:17). If you believe God is your provider, give generously. If you believe God is your peace, lay down your anxiety. Prove what you believe by how you behave today.
